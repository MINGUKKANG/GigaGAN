Abstract

The recent success of text-to-image synthesis has taken the world by storm and captured the general public's imagination. From a technical standpoint, it also marked a drastic change in the favored architecture to design image generative models. GANs used to be the de facto choice, e.g., with techniques like StyleGAN. With DALL·E 2, auto-regressive and diffusion models became the new standard for large-scale generative models overnight. This rapid shift leaves a fundamental question open: can GANs similarly scale up to sizes that benefit from large datasets like LAION? We find that naÏvely increasing the capacity of the StyleGAN architecture quickly becomes unstable. We introduce a new GAN architecture that goes well above this limit and we demonstrate that GANs are a viable option for text-to-image synthesis. Our approach has major practical advantages: it is orders of magnitude faster at inference time, supports latent interpolation and style changes.
